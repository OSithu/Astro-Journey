Solar System

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the solar system. It has a rocky surface covered with craters, resembling the Moon, and experiences extreme temperature fluctuations due to its thin atmosphere. With no moons, Mercury is known for its rapid orbit, completing a revolution around the Sun in just 88 Earth days.

Special: Closest to the Sun

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only one known to support life. It has a breathable atmosphere, abundant water, and diverse ecosystems. Earth has one large natural satellite, the Moon, and its environment is uniquely suited to sustain a wide variety of life forms.Earth's biosphere fosters incredible biological diversity. Life thrives here.

Special: Only supports life

More Details

Mars

Mars, the Red Planet, is the fourth from the Sun. Its reddish hue comes from iron oxide on its surface. Mars has striking geological features, including the largest volcano (Olympus Mons) and the deepest canyon system (Valles Marineris). The planet has a thin atmosphere and two small moons, Phobos and Deimos.

Special: Red iron oxide

More Details

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, with a composition primarily of hydrogen and helium. It is known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has persisted for centuries. Jupiter has an extensive system of moons, with the Galilean moons—Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto—being the most well-known.

Special: Great Red Spot

More Details

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its magnificent ring system made of ice and rock particles. It is the second-largest planet and, like Jupiter, is predominantly composed of hydrogen and helium. Saturn has many moons, with Titan being the largest, known for its thick atmosphere and methane lakes.Saturn's rings encircle the gas giant.

Special: Spectacular ring system

Uranus

Uranus is unique in that it rotates on its side, possibly due to a past collision. This axial tilt leads to extreme seasonal variations. Its blue-green color comes from methane in its atmosphere. Uranus has several moons, with Miranda and Ariel among the notable ones.
